Cab Calloway

Cab Calloway (born December 25, 1907 and died November 18, 1994), was an American jazz singer, bandleader, and entertainer known for his energetic performances and scat singing. Leading The Cotton Club Orchestra, later renamed Cab Calloway and His Orchestra, he became a swing-era icon. His hit Minnie the Moocher was the first jazz record to sell over a million copies. Calloway's flamboyant style and smooth vocals left a lasting impact on jazz and entertainment.
